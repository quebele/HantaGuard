Understanding Hantavirus

Vital information on Hantavirus Pulmonary Syndrome (HPS) and how to protect your home and family.

What is Hantavirus?

Hantaviruses are a family of viruses spread mainly by rodents. While rare, they can cause serious diseases in humans, most notably Hantavirus Pulmonary Syndrome (HPS).

As of 2026, awareness remains the primary tool for prevention, as the virus is highly resilient in dusty, enclosed environments like sheds, cabins, and crawlspaces.

Key Facts

  • ✔ Not spread between humans.
  • ✔ Carried by specific wild rodents (e.g., Deer Mouse).
  • ✔ Can be fatal if left untreated.

How It Spreads

🌬️

Airborne

Inhaling dust contaminated with rodent urine, droppings, or saliva (aerosolization).

🤝

Direct Contact

Touching contaminated materials and then touching your mouth, nose, or eyes.

🐭

Bites

Though rare, being bitten by an infected rodent can transmit the virus.

Early & Late Symptoms

Early Stage (Days 1-5)

  • Fatigue and Fever
  • Muscle aches (especially thighs, hips, back)
  • Headaches and Dizziness
  • Chills and Abdominal problems

Late Stage (Days 4-10)

  • Coughing and Shortness of breath
  • Tightness around the chest
  • Fluid in the lungs
  • Respiratory failure
Medical Alert: If you have been around rodents and experience shortness of breath, seek emergency medical care immediately.

Prevention is Key

01

Seal Up

Seal gaps in and around your home to prevent rodents from entering. Use steel wool or caulk.

02

Trap Up

Use snap traps in areas where rodents are likely to be found. Keep areas clean of food sources.

03

Clean Up (Safely)

Never sweep or vacuum rodent droppings. Wet the area with disinfectant or a 10% bleach solution before cleaning.
