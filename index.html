What is Hantavirus?

Hantaviruses are viruses carried by rodents. Infection can happen when people breathe in contaminated dust or come into contact with urine, droppings, saliva, or nesting materials from infected animals.

Symptoms

Early symptoms

  • Fever
  • Headache
  • Muscle aches
  • Fatigue
  • Nausea or vomiting
  • Stomach pain or diarrhea

Serious warning signs

  • Shortness of breath
  • Coughing
  • Chest tightness
  • Rapid worsening after flu-like symptoms
Breathing problems after possible rodent exposure need urgent medical evaluation.

How it spreads

Hantavirus usually spreads from rodents to humans, not from person to person. Risk increases when enclosed spaces are disturbed, especially if rodent droppings or nesting material are present.

People often encounter the virus while cleaning sheds, opening storage areas, or handling contaminated material in rural or poorly ventilated locations.

Common risk situations

  • Cleaning old storage spaces
  • Sweeping dry rodent droppings
  • Handling nests or contaminated debris
  • Working in closed, dusty areas

Prevention

Keep rodents out

  • Seal cracks, gaps, and entry holes
  • Store food in sealed containers
  • Reduce clutter where rodents can nest
  • Use traps where appropriate

Clean safely

  • Ventilate enclosed areas first
  • Do not sweep dry droppings
  • Wear gloves and a mask when cleaning
  • Disinfect contaminated surfaces carefully

When to seek medical care

Hantavirus has no specific cure or vaccine. Treatment is supportive and may require hospital care, especially if breathing problems or other severe symptoms appear.
