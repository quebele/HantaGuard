What is Hantavirus?

Hantaviruses are viruses carried by rodents. People can become infected through contact with rodent urine, droppings, saliva, or nesting materials, especially when contaminated dust is stirred into the air. The illness can begin like the flu, which makes early recognition important.

On this page, visitors can quickly learn the basics, compare common symptoms, and understand practical steps that reduce exposure risk at home, work, or in outdoor spaces.

At a glance

Symptoms

Common early signs

  • Fever
  • Headache
  • Muscle aches
  • Fatigue
  • Nausea or vomiting
  • Stomach pain or diarrhea

Severe warning signs

  • Shortness of breath
  • Coughing
  • Chest tightness
  • Rapid worsening after flu-like symptoms
If breathing problems start after possible rodent exposure, medical attention should be sought immediately.

How it spreads

Hantavirus usually spreads from rodents to people. Risk increases when people clean enclosed or poorly ventilated areas, handle rodent waste, or disturb contaminated materials. Bites are less common, but they can also be a route of exposure.

The best traffic-generating pages answer the question users are actually asking: what is it, how does it spread, what are the signs, and what should I do next? This layout keeps those answers above the fold and easy to scan.

Common exposure points

  • Cabins and storage spaces
  • Garages and attics
  • Barns, sheds, and basements
  • Camp sites and rural buildings

Prevention

Reduce rodent access

  • Seal gaps, cracks, and entry holes
  • Store food in sealed containers
  • Remove clutter where rodents can nest
  • Use traps and keep problem areas clean

Clean safely

  • Ventilate enclosed areas first
  • Do not sweep dry droppings
  • Use protective gloves and a mask
  • Disinfect contaminated surfaces carefully

Treatment

There is no specific cure for hantavirus. Treatment is supportive and may require hospital care, especially if breathing or kidney problems develop. Early medical evaluation can make a big difference.
