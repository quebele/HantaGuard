Tip 1
Spot symptoms early
Watch for fever, muscle aches, and breathing problems after possible rodent exposure.
Tip 2
Clean safely
Do not sweep dry droppings. Ventilate first and disinfect contaminated areas carefully.
Tip 3
Keep rodents out
Seal entry points, store food securely, and keep storage areas uncluttered.

Frequently Asked Questions

Hantavirus is usually spread from rodents to humans. Person-to-person transmission is uncommon and depends on the virus type.
It can spread when someone breathes in contaminated dust or contacts urine, droppings, saliva, or nesting material from infected rodents.
No specific vaccine or cure is available. Treatment is supportive, and severe cases may require hospital care.
Watch for symptoms for several weeks, and seek medical advice quickly if flu-like symptoms appear or worsen.
